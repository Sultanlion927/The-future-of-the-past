UAE the country of future

UAE started small with the land of the desert whose living was based on fishing villages and date farms. There were no schools, hospitals and even proper housing structures to plan a life there. People used to travel to India and other countries for their medical treatment. There used to be only two to three schools and the students had to wait from the Fajar time in the cold evenings to catch the van. Life was quite tough. When the 20th century unfolded in UAE, Abu Dhabi and Dubai were the poorest emirates while Sharjah was powerful. But still, the living was highly dependent on fishing villages, pearling, camel herding, and farming in the oasis. However, all that took a 360-degree shift when oil was first discovered in UAE in 1958 following a 30-year search. After the discovery of oil, UAE started its journey of development at a gradual pace. The first thing to come into being was the starting of the exports to the other countries. It became so successful that it turned Abu Dhabi into the richest country and gave fuel to the operations within the city. Now, Dubai and Abu Dhabi are two major cities which add 70 to 80 percent to the economy. With the rise in the oil and natural gas transportation, the education sector also starts evolving. There was a time when only mosques were the place to learn and students had been taught basic Arabic, Maths and Grammar there. But now the basic education is free for all students including the international students who come to UAE for studies. The revenue generated from the oil also helps UAE to boost the construction, health and tourism industry. Dubai is one of the beautiful cities which is known for its tallest buildings, serene beaches and heart captivating locations. People come from far and wide to visit Dubai which has also boosted the tourism culture in Dubai. Plus, the festivities in the UAE also attracts people from all over the world. All this progress happened because of the strong vision of the UAE which started from the reign of Sheikh Zayed. In the past years from 1990 to 2000, UAE has imported around US$54.2 billion for each year. Now, in UAE there are various schools and hospitals on which you can rely on your education and healthcare. Apart from the schools, there are also additional services like essay writing in Dubai from which you can seek help from your academics. Although the past of UAE has been dark with the lack of resources to grow but now UAE is the major attraction for the business and tourism culture. It’s also known as one of the brightest and safest countries in the world. The highest capita income in UAE is around $25,000 USD. Apart from this, it has approximately 100 percent of the world’s total oil reserves, 90 percent in Abu Dhabi and 10 percent in Dubai. Now, the UAE no longer depends solely on oil and gas revenue. Their trade, tourism, real estate, and construction work are also profit-generating in Dubai.

Before the Emirates

For centuries the region was mired in rivalries between local emirs on land while pirates scoured the seas and used the states’ shores as their refuge.
Britain began attacking pirates to protect its trade with India.
That led to British ties with the Trucial States’ emirs. The ties were formalized in 1820 after Britain offered protection in exchange for exclusivity: the emirs, accepting a truce brokered by Britain, pledged not to cede any land to ​any powers or make any treaties with anyone except Britain. They also agreed to settle subsequent disputes through British authorities. The subservient relationship was to last a century and a half,
until 1971.
